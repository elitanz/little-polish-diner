Our Story

A 22-seat hole-in-the-wall in Polish Village.

Since 2006, The Little Polish Diner has served the real thing on Ridge Road, in the heart of Parma's historic Polish Village. It's tiny — 22 seats — and the regulars just call the sampler "a plate."

Sophie Hart and the kitchen make it the old way: pierogi pan-fried in butter and onions, golabki, and a pot of borscht. Smacznego!

See the menu
First —

Six kinds of pierogi.

Potato & cheddar, sauerkraut, mushroom, meat, and sweet cheese — pan-fried with onions, like Babcia's. Locals just call it "a plate."

Then —

Golabki & kielbasa.

Stuffed cabbage in tomato, kielbasa & kraut, a breaded pork chop — Polish comfort, plated generous.

And —

A bowl of borscht.

Ruby-red beet borscht, and breaded lake perch every Friday. Bring an appetite and a little patience.
